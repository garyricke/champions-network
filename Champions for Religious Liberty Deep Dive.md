**The Champions for Religious Liberty Network: A Comprehensive Strategic Deep Dive**

The Champions for Religious Liberty Network is a specialized advocacy initiative of the **Lutheran Center for Religious Liberty (LCRL)**. Based in Washington, D.C., the network is designed to move beyond traditional church growth models to focus on educating and empowering "Common People"—lay congregants and citizens—to engage American culture through a specific theological and constitutional lens.

## **I. The Core Problem: Identified Cultural Threats**

The LCRL identifies a "powerful problem" in America where Christianity is being maligned and "criminalized". The network explicitly addresses several "progressive humanist" shifts:

* **Redefining Marriage and Gender:** The "re-defining" of marriage and the "re-gendering" of humans.

* **Sanctity of Life:** The "cheapening" or "limiting" of human life to pragmatic standards.

* **Government Overreach:** A trend where the government is perceived to be replacing both God and parental authority.

* **Secular Blasphemy Laws:** The rise of policies that penalize biblical worldviews as "hate speech".

## **II. Theological Framework: Two-Kingdom Citizenship**

The network's strategy is built on **Two-Kingdom Theology**, which distinguishes between God's two distinct ways of working in the world:

### **1\. The Left-Hand Kingdom (The Kingdom of Power/Preserving)**

* **Purpose:** To preserve temporal civility, curb evil, and maintain peace through reason and law.  
* **Role—Champion Public Servants:** Christians act as parents, citizens, and law enforcement to preserve the nation's biblical foundations.

### **2\. The Right-Hand Kingdom (The Kingdom of Grace/Saving)**

* **Purpose:** To save souls through the Gospel and the power of the Word.  
* **Role—Champion Witnesses:** Christians spread the message of salvation and the freedom found in Christ.

## **III. The Five-Year Developmental Pathway (Tiers)**

To ensure advocacy is grounded in maturity rather than "reactionary activism," the LCRL utilizes a tiered "journeyman" system.

| Tier | Designation | Key Objectives & Milestones |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **T1** | **Apprentice** | Learning foundational principles of religious liberty and Two-Kingdom citizenship. |
| **T2** | **Journeyman (Squire)** | Refining applications and engaging local, state, and federal governing authorities. |
| **T3** | **Journeyman (Bachelor)** | Maturing into an "advocate" who engages culture for the sake of the Church’s mission without being a political activist. |
| **T4** | **Journeyman (Banneret)** | Mentoring newer members; demonstrating how to "depoliticize" public issues; participating in national LCRL events. |
| **T5** | **Knight (First Class)** | Reaching a status of long-term loyalty; mentoring new cohorts; using the "Knight" symbol (inspired by the angel Gabriel). |

## **IV. The "Toolchest": Curricular and Operational Depth**

Member groups, known as **Champions Chapters**, are provided with a "toolkit" of resources:

* **Curriculum Structure:** 10 sessions per year featuring video devotions (e.g., "Bearing the Sword for Good Reason"), discussion guides, and tutorial videos.

* **Required Reading:** Deep dives into Luke Goodrich's book, *Free To Believe*.

* **Mom & Pop Papers:** Specialized guides for current events and community engagement.

* **Chapter Leadership:** Each chapter is led by **4–5 Chapter Facilitators** (Hospitality, Prayer, and Communication) to ensure the workload is shared.

## **V. Implementation: Events and Getting Started**

Affiliation typically begins with a high-impact introductory event.

### **1\. Liberty Lectures**

* **Option 1 (One-Hour):** A 60–90 minute interactive presentation on constitutional protections.  
* **Option 3 (Four-Hour "Primer"):** Usually a Saturday morning (10:00 am – 2:00 pm) featuring three sessions: foundations, principles, and engagement strategies.

### **2\. Liberty Weekends**

A comprehensive event where LCRL staff (typically **Rev. Dr. Gregory Seltz** and **Rev. Mark Frith**) conduct the Saturday workshop and lead Sunday preaching and Bible classes.
