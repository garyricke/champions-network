Who We Are

Common People. Extraordinary Purpose.

The Champions Network is a vibrant community learning, growing, praying, and challenging one another to engage their local communities with a Two-Kingdom set of principles.

Common People

Congregants & Citizens Who Love the LORD

The Champions Network is built for everyday believers — not politicians or professional activists. Congregants, citizens, and people who love the LORD and the liberties that He alone can provide.

Our mission is to inspire and equip individuals dedicated to making a difference in their congregations, schools, and communities. We seek to put our temporal liberties to work for the greater, eternal liberties of the Gospel for all.

Congregants in local Lutheran and evangelical churches
Citizens who value the First Amendment and biblical freedom
Lay people seeking a 5-year path to principled engagement

The Champions Journey

A Five-Year Developmental Pathway

To ensure advocacy is grounded in maturity rather than reactionary activism, the LCRL utilizes a tiered journeyman system. The calling continues until Jesus returns.

Year 1 · Apprentice
T1 — The Champions Apprentice Year

Begin the Journey

The first year focuses on learning the foundational principles of religious liberty and Two-Kingdom citizenship. You'll discover how to engage as a 2KG Christian — earning dialogue opportunities before rushing engagement.

Foundational principles of religious liberty
Introduction to Two-Kingdom citizenship
Community engagement best practices
10 group sessions with video devotions & discussion guides
Year 2 · Squire
T2 — The Champions Journeyman: Squire

Refine & Expand

Year two deepens your application of Two-Kingdom principles. You begin engaging local, state, and federal governing authorities with confidence and wisdom — and may begin guiding new cohorts through Year 1.

Refining Two-Kingdom application in real contexts
Engaging local and state governing authorities

Continued Mom & Pop Papers and community action items

T3 — The Champions Journeyman: Bachelor

Mature into an Advocate

Year three marks a shift from learner to advocate. You discover how to gather information about culture and governing policies, engaging for cultural benefit and the church's mission — without becoming a partisan activist.

Maturation in advocacy — not activism

Focus on taking politics off of the issues

Mentoring T1–T3 cohort members

The Toolchest

Champions Academy

Each group in our network is called a Champions Chapter. We provide educational resources, digital delivery systems, discussion guides, and regular communication support — so that many hands make for light work.

Resources are batched into Session Themes — 10 sessions per year. Chapters facilitate and adapt each session meeting for their participants, choosing to meet at least once a month (though many meet more frequently). Each session includes a Group Discussion Guide with tutorial videos and one or more discussion handouts.
Each Chapter is enrolled with a toolkit filled with educational resources and communication apps that allow for excellence in staying connected with Network Staff and Facilitators of other Champion Chapters across the country. Session Learning Modules are always at your fingertips.
Each session also includes Home Study Guides that supplement the group time, allowing each participant to increase their knowledge independently. We encourage participants to read a little each day and slowly build their ability to one day become a great advocate. Required reading includes Free to Believe by Luke Goodrich.
The group facilitates a Community Action Plan through the first year, assigning easy objectives to participants. The key to being a faithful Two-Kingdom advocate is prayerfully seeking to differentiate just how God may be engaging people in the lefthand kingdom — which assumes Champions are doing a lot of listening and praying before engaging.
Specialized guides for current events and community engagement — practical tools that help Champions understand what is happening in their local community and governing structures, and how to apply Two-Kingdom principles in real time without becoming reactive or partisan.
Practical conversation guides help Champions engage difficult topics — life, marriage, educational freedom, and religious liberty — with winsome confidence. The goal: "speak truth in love" in ways that inspire genuine dialogue rather than entrench opposition.

Seeking to be useful in God's hands, Champions seek:

Our Focus

Four Areas of Concern

The Champions Network focuses its educational resources and community engagement around four core areas where religious liberty is most directly threatened.

Right of Conscience & Religious Liberty

Defending every Christian's First Amendment right to live, work, and worship according to their faith without government penalty.

Marriage & the Family

Upholding marriage as God's institution and defending the family as the cornerstone of both Church and civic society.

Sanctity of Life

Affirming that every human being is made in the image of God, opposing the cheapening of life to pragmatic or utilitarian standards.

Educational Freedom

Protecting parental rights and school choice, opposing a public education system increasingly hostile to the Judeo-Christian worldview.

Champions Chapters

Set Free — To Live Freely Together

Each Champions Chapter is led by 4–5 Chapter Facilitators — ensuring the workload is shared and many hands make for light work in leading the Chapter.

Chapter Facilitator

Leads and coordinates the overall chapter, ensuring sessions run smoothly and participants stay engaged.

Prayer Facilitator

Leads the chapter in prayer, grounding all engagement in humility and dependence on the Holy Spirit.

Hospitality Facilitator

Creates a welcoming environment where members feel at home, encouraged to participate and return.

Communication Facilitator

Manages outreach, coordination, and connectivity with other chapters and the broader LCRL network.

Pastoral Advisor

The chapter's pastor provides theological grounding, accountability, and support for all chapter activities.

Area of Concern

Redefining Marriage & Gender

The systematic re-definition of marriage and the re-gendering of humans represents one of the most direct challenges to the biblical worldview in American public life. These shifts are not merely cultural — they carry legal weight that directly affects Christians in employment, education, and ministry.

"So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them." — Genesis 1:27

The Champions Network equips members to understand these issues theologically, engage them with winsome conviction, and protect the church's freedom to preach and teach God's design for marriage and human identity.

  • Understanding the legal landscape post-Obergefell and gender identity mandates
  • How to speak truthfully and lovingly in your community
  • Protecting church schools and institutions
Area of Concern

Sanctity of Life

The cheapening and limiting of human life to pragmatic or utilitarian standards is a direct attack on the foundational truth that every person is made in the image of God — from conception to natural death.

"Before I formed you in the womb I knew you, before you were born I set you apart." — Jeremiah 1:5

Champions learn to engage pro-life issues not as political activists but as Two-Kingdom advocates — understanding both the civil dimension of protecting the vulnerable and the Gospel dimension of proclaiming the value of every soul.

  • Biblical and constitutional foundations for life advocacy
  • Engaging the community without partisan reactivity
  • Supporting families and crisis pregnancy ministries
Area of Concern

Government Overreach

A dangerous trend in American governance seeks to replace both God and parental authority with state power. From mandated curriculum in public schools to regulatory pressure on faith-based organizations, the Left-Hand Kingdom is being weaponized against the institutions God established.

"We must obey God rather than human beings." — Acts 5:29

Two-Kingdom Champions understand the proper role of government — limited, accountable, and subject to God's higher authority — and learn to engage governing authorities with respect and principled conviction.

  • The American experiment and its unique constitutional design
  • Parental rights and school choice advocacy
  • How to engage city, state, and federal officials as a Christian
Area of Concern

Secular Blasphemy Laws

The rise of policies, institutional norms, and legal mechanisms that penalize biblical worldviews as "hate speech" is the most direct threat to free religious expression in America today. Christianity is being quietly criminalized in the public square.

"For it is God's will that by doing good you should silence the ignorant talk of foolish people." — 1 Peter 2:15

Champions learn to identify these encroachments, understand their legal rights, and respond not with anger but with the winsome, truth-filled engagement that the Gospel produces in the hearts of Two-Kingdom citizens.

  • First Amendment protections for speech, press, and religion
  • How "hate speech" frameworks undermine free exercise
  • LCRL's legal resources and advocacy on Capitol Hill
Theological Foundation

Two-Kingdom Theology: A Deeper Look

Two-Kingdom Theology is the Lutheran theological framework that distinguishes between God's two distinct modes of governing the world — a distinction that is foundational to everything the Champions Network does.

"Let every person be subject to the governing authorities. For there is no authority except from God, and those that exist have been instituted by God." — Romans 13:1

The Left-Hand Kingdom (Kingdom of Power/Preserving): God works through secular institutions — government, law, family, vocation — to curb evil, maintain civil peace, and preserve the temporal blessings He has given. Christians serve here as parents, citizens, employers, and public servants.

The Right-Hand Kingdom (Kingdom of Grace/Saving): God works through the Church and the Word to save souls through faith in Jesus Christ. This is the Church's unique and eternal mission — what the Left-Hand Kingdom ultimately exists to protect space for.

The Champions Network trains members in Two-Kingdom Differentiation, Two-Kingdom Restraint, Two-Kingdom Respect, and Two-Kingdom Guided Engagement — so that advocacy blesses both the community and the church's saving mission.
