Hello, Champion Citizens, Pastor Mark Frith with you again for a quick few thoughts on Chapter 2 of Luke Goodriches’ book Free To Believe.  Chapter 2 is titled HOW TO GET IT RIGHT.  Should we presume that he’s talking about Religious Liberty?  Well, of course. 

Religious Liberty is not defined by the Greeks, The Romans, nor the American Constituion.  That is important to realize.  As Goodrich states, Religious Liberty is rooted in God’s design for humanity.  And this chapter will lead you through the evidence for this.  You will want to spend some time taking this to heart. Secondly we learn that God is a relationship being.  And Scripture tells us time and time again, that while humans turn away from Him, He never stops pursuing them with a desire to win their hearts. Enjoy a couple of pages where Goodrich reminds you of God’s pursuits.  Learn some of them so that you can share with others. 

Third point in this chapter is that being made in His image, there is a “thirsting for God’ in our being.  I love this aspect. I read and reread this section. It quenched my thirst for knowledge. Note the references biblically provided by Goodrich.  Underline them in your book.  

Goodrich asks the question: Why did God give us the ability to reject him?  Take a peek at that section.  It is the greatest act of love.  If you forced someone to be with you, do you think that would work?  Would that person love you back?  The answer to that is NOPE. Think about God and His desire for us, but won’t coerce us. 

Next, the book draws us into a deeper understanding of justice.  Religious Liberty and justice, interesting relationship.  What is the union of this?  Again, as we are made in the image of God, and there is a desire that God and people come together.  Being like God, means reflecting Him.  And a part of that reflection is our relationship with other people.  

God and you is a vertical relationship.  Human to human is a horizontal relationship.  We have a responsibility to do well by one another. Justice metes out punishment for the wrongdoer and protection for the most vulnerable.

We’re going to sew these teachings so close to our hearts and minds that you are going to be a blessing as a Lefthand Kingdom citizen, a Champion of religious liberty.

Dig into those couple of pages and how we can’t use Israel’s form of government as a template for ours. 

Finally, enjoy the three objections as arguments in this chapter: Objections about Israel. Objections about Heretics and governments inability to play that role.  Objections about Limits and the appearance of religious freedom that seems to have no limits.  Goodrich is going to discuss this objection extensively later in the book.

The Author - Luke Goodrich is a lawyer who works to protect religious freedom. He works for a group called the Becket Fund that helps people's religious rights. Luke has won important court cases at the Supreme Court defending people's religious beliefs, including nuns and a Muslim prisoner. He frequently talks about religious freedom on TV and in newspaper articles. Luke teaches law students about the Constitution too. Before working at Becket, Luke was a law clerk. He also went to law school at the University of Chicago where he did very well. Through his work and writing, Luke has become a leading expert on religious liberty issues in America.

The Reason for the Book - Goodrich wrote the book "Free to Believe" to explore the growing threats to religious liberty in America today. He looks at how religious freedom has been a core right throughout U.S. history, but now faces increasing legal challenges and societal changes that jeopardize its protection.

The book informs readers on the importance of religious freedom by providing insights into real-life legal battles involving individuals and groups whose religious practices have faced obstacles. It aims to engage both legal experts and the general public in understanding the complexities surrounding religious liberty in our modern society.

In the introduction Luke shares the story of Hosanna Lutheran Church and School, LCMS, in Redford, Michigan, who was sued by a former school teacher, a suit that went all the way to the Supreme Court.  You will enjoy that story, and need to appreciate that story.  

The lawsuits today against Christians are far more vitriolic and look to be unending. It is only a matter of time before it just may strike close to home for you.  When it comes to same-sex marriage, transgendered care, what pastors preach, what Christians universities teach, who sits in your church, who occuplies your classroom, all of these topics and arenas are being primed for future confrontation.

For now those who are prime subjects today are the laymen and laywomen, the business owners who run their businesses and organizations with a Christ-centered focus, for all the world to see.  These are the Christians who are suffering immediate attacks today.  Even police officers and fire marshalls, school officials, all of these people who do not embrace a certain idiology are subject to discipline and are often fired.
