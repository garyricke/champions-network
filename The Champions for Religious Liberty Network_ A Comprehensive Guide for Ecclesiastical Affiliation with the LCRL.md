# **The Champions for Religious Liberty Network: Strategic Advocacy and Two-Kingdom Citizenship**

The Champions for Religious Liberty Network is a specialized initiative of the Lutheran Center for Religious Liberty (LCRL) based in Washington, D.C..1 Unlike traditional ecclesiastical networks focused on growth or denominational branding, the LCRL network is designed to educate and empower Christians to engage American culture and government through a "Two-Kingdom" biblical worldview.1 For churches considering affiliation, the network offers a structured, multi-year pathway to transform congregants into informed "Champion Citizens" who can advocate for religious freedom, marriage, and the sanctity of life.

## **The Philosophical Foundation: Two-Kingdom Theology**

The network’s core identity is rooted in the "Two-Kingdom" perspective, a theological framework that distinguishes between the two ways God interacts with the world.3

* **The Left-Hand Kingdom (Preserving Work):** God works through secular government, law enforcement, and parental authority to curb evil and maintain temporal civility.  
* **The Right-Hand Kingdom (Saving Work):** God works through the Church and the proclamation of the Gospel to save souls through faith in Christ.

A primary goal for member churches is to help congregants "dynamically differentiate" these roles. By understanding that the state’s purpose is to preserve while the Church’s purpose is to save, Christians can engage in public affairs without confusing temporal political success with spiritual salvation.

## **The Structural Model: From Academy to Network**

Affiliation with the LCRL is organized into three distinct operational layers, allowing churches to scale their involvement as they develop internal leadership.

### **1\. The 2KG Academy**

The entry point for individuals and small groups, providing educational resources and discussions on the Two-Kingdom perspective.1 It features tracks for general citizens and advanced tracks for those developing as local leaders.1

### **2\. The Champions Church**

A church becomes an "Official Champions Church" after successfully completing the first year of the 2KG Academy.1 In this stage, the LCRL works directly with pastors and church boards to provide strategic support for their "Champion Citizens" and community engagement plans.

### **3\. The Champions Network**

The highest level of affiliation, functioning as a nationwide coalition of Champions Churches.1 This layer allows congregations to share ideas, connect across state lines, and speak with a unified voice on national policies affecting religious liberty and education.

## **The Five-Year Developmental Pathway (Tiers)**

Potential member churches must understand that the network utilizes a tiered "journeyman" system to ensure that advocacy is grounded in maturity rather than reactionary activism.

| Tier | Designation | Year | Focus |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **T1** | Apprentice | 1 | Learning the foundational principles of religious liberty and Two-Kingdom citizenship.3 |
| **T2** | Journeyman (Squire) | 2 | Refining applications and beginning to engage local and state governing authorities.3 |
| **T3** | Journeyman (Bachelor) | 3 | Maturing in community engagement and discovering ways to communicate without being "activists".3 |
| **T4** | Journeyman (Banneret) | 4 | Mentoring newer members and participating in regional/national LCRL events.3 |
| **T5** | Knight (First Class) | 5+ | Long-term loyalty and mentoring the next generation of Champion Citizens. |

The use of "Knighthood" and the angel Gabriel as a symbol signifies the network’s commitment to "defending the preaching of the Gospel".

## **Leadership and Governance**

The network is overseen by seasoned leaders with deep roots in the Lutheran Church—Missouri Synod (LCMS) and public policy.

* **Rev. Dr. Gregory Seltz:** Executive Director of the LCRL. Seltz provides the visionary leadership and theological grounding, often hosting the *Liberty Action Alert* podcast to discuss legal and cultural crises.  
* **Rev. Mark Frith:** Network Director of the Champions for Religious Liberty. Frith focuses on the practical recruitment and support of churches, bringing experience as a former LCMS pastor and missions assistant.

Member churches remain autonomous in their governance but agree to "lock arms" with the LCRL in defending shared values.3 The network emphasizes becoming "advocates" (who engage for the sake of the mission) rather than "activists" (who may prioritize political outcomes over Gospel proclamation).3

## **Joining Process and Membership Requirements**

For a church to join the network, it must move through a deliberate introductory and training phase.3

### **Initial Engagement Options**

* **Liberty Lectures:** 60-90 minute interactive presentations on foundational constitutional protections and biblical roots of freedom.3  
* **Liberty Weekends:** Comprehensive events featuring a Saturday workshop and a Sunday "Religious Liberty Sunday" with LCRL staff preaching and leading Bible classes.

### **Steps for Affiliation**

1. **Form a Leadership Team:** The church identifies a core group of members ready to lead the movement locally.1  
2. **Launch the 2KG Academy:** The church recruits participants from the congregation to begin the Tier 1 curriculum.1  
3. **Establish Partnership:** After Year 1, the church formally partners with the LCRL as a "Champions Church".1  
4. **Virtual Consultation:** Arranging a meeting with the Champion's Director, Rev. Mark Frith, to discuss localized engagement strategies.3

## **Costs, Fees, and Benefits**

### **Financial Considerations**

While some introductory events, such as a "Liberty Weekend" at specific host churches, may be free to attend with registration, the LCRL acknowledges that there are "some costs involved" in the full partnership and 2KG Academy implementation. These costs typically cover educational resources and ongoing support from the LCRL staff.1

### **Strategic Benefits for the Local Church**

* **Unified Voice:** Connecting with a nationwide coalition to speak on key issues like marriage and education.1  
* **Resource Access:** Receiving input, education, advice, and advocacy resources directly from the LCRL’s office in Washington, D.C..  
* **Leadership Development:** Providing a clear, meaningful pathway for laypeople to serve as "Champion Citizens" within their own communities.1  
* **Cultural Resilience:** Equipping members to handle cultural vilification by speaking the "truth in love" with a biblically-informed worldview.

## **Conclusion: A Mission-Driven Partnership**

The Champions for Religious Liberty Network offers a unique value proposition for churches concerned about the "progressive humanist" shift in American culture. By focusing on the Two-Kingdom doctrine, the LCRL ensures that member churches can preserve the "temporal civility and peace" of the nation without compromising the saving mission of the Church. Potential members should view joining not as a simple membership, but as a long-term commitment to mentoring "Knights" who stand in the gap for the heart of individual freedoms.3
