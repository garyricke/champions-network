### **Champions for Religious Liberty Network — Brand Standards Guide**

**An Initiative of the Lutheran Center for Religious Liberty (LCRL) | Washington, D.C.**

## ---

**1\. Brand Overview**

### **Organization & Mission**

The **Champions for Religious Liberty Network** is a specialized initiative of the **Lutheran Center for Religious Liberty (LCRL)**. It moves beyond traditional church growth models to focus on educating and empowering lay congregants—"Common People"—to engage American culture through a specific theological and constitutional lens. The mission is to form **Champion Citizens** who understand Two-Kingdom citizenship and can advocate for religious liberty, marriage, and the sanctity of life.

### **Core Identity: Two-Kingdom Theology**

The brand is built on the **Two-Kingdom** framework, distinguishing God’s distinct ways of working in the world:

* **The Left-Hand Kingdom (Kingdom of Power/Preserving):** God works through secular government, reason, and law to curb evil and maintain peace.  
* **The Right-Hand Kingdom (Kingdom of Grace/Saving):** God works through the Church and the Gospel to save souls through faith in Christ.

## ---

**2\. Strategic Context: Identified Threats**

The network explicitly addresses cultural shifts that the LCRL identifies as "criminalizing" Christianity:

* **Redefining Marriage and Gender:** Opposing the "re-gendering" of humans and redefinition of marriage.  
* **Sanctity of Life:** Addressing the "cheapening" of human life.  
* **Government Overreach:** Addressing trends where the government replaces parental or divine authority.  
* **Secular Blasphemy Laws:** Opposing policies that penalize biblical worldviews as "hate speech".

## ---

**3\. The Five-Tier Developmental Pathway**

The network uses a "journeyman" system to ensure advocacy is grounded in maturity.

| Tier | Designation | Year | Focus & Milestones |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **T1** | **Apprentice** | 1 | Foundational principles of religious liberty and Two-Kingdom citizenship. |
| **T2** | **Journeyman (Squire)** | 2 | Refining applications; engaging local/state governing authorities. |
| **T3** | **Journeyman (Bachelor)** | 3 | Maturing as an advocate; communicating conviction without "activism". |
| **T4** | **Journeyman (Banneret)** | 4 | Mentoring new members; participating in national LCRL events. |
| **T5** | **Knight (First Class)** | 5+ | Long-term loyalty; mentoring new cohorts using the "Knight" symbol. |

## ---

**4\. Visual Identity & Typography**

### **Logo Design Elements**

* **Symbol:** A clean shield outline containing an upward-pointing sword. The tip features a gold star-cross hybrid, representing the "Saving" purpose.  
* **Primary Typeface (The Constraint):** Replicates the **unique, condensed serif** from the parent LCRL logo to ensure immediate brand association.

### **Typography Specifications**

* **Primary Brand/Headlines:** Use the **LCRL Parent Serif** (Condensed, Tan/Gold) for "CHAMPIONS NETWORK".  
* **Secondary/Subtext:** Use a **Modern Geometric Sans-Serif** in light or thin weights.  
  * *Recommended:* **Proxima Nova Light** or **Poppins Light**.  
  * *Alternative:* **Montserrat Light**.  
* **Hierarchy:** Use the serif for authority and the sans-serif for clarity in fine print and body copy.

## ---

**6\. Operational Structure & Voice**

### **Voice Pillars**

* **Theologically Precise:** Distinguish the two kingdoms; never conflate political victory with the Church's mission.  
* **Advocates, Not Activists:** Measured, truth-filled engagement motivated by love, not reactionary combat.  
* **Formative, Not Reactionary:** Focus on the long-term 5-year arc rather than alarm-driven messaging.

### **Implementation Tools**

* **2KG Academy:** Entry point providing educational resources for individuals.  
* **Champions Chapters:** Led by 4–5 facilitators focusing on Hospitality, Prayer, and Communication.  
* **Required Reading:** *Free To Believe* by Luke Goodrich.  
* **Initial Engagement:** **Liberty Lectures** (1–4 hour primers) or **Liberty Weekends** (Workshops \+ Sunday preaching).
